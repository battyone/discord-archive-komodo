Is the order book decentralized? Are orders placed off-chain? As a user, do I need to trust third parties to find out the price and to find the best possible counter-offers? (edited)

gcharang 25-Dec-19 04:42 PM
Is the order book decentralized? Are orders placed off-chain? As a user, do I need to trust third parties to find out the price and to find the best possible counter-offers?
the order book is decentralized and p2p more info here: https://developers.komodoplatform.com/basic-docs/start-here/core-technology-discussions/atomicdex.html#the-decentralized-orderbook
Would it be possible for a single order to be matched against multiple counter-offers in the future or there is a fundamental reason why it must be 1-to-1?
a maker order, once placed, does allow multiple takers who need lower volumes to fill it but, it might happen with some delay as each taker's offer has to be filled by hash and timelocks on a blockchain(for utxo coins) and some confirmations are needed for confident trading
jong 25-Dec-19 05:03 PM
@gcharang Thanks, that sounds good. Can the order go through partially if one of the counterparties is offline/slow?
or it's an all-or-nothing situation
gcharang 25-Dec-19 05:05 PM
if one party is slow, the order can still go through (the blockchains have blocktimes and confirmation requirements for safe trading ) but if one of them is offline, the swap won't go through
the taker and maker need to execute alternate steps if one of them doesn't execute a step, the swap timesout/fails and the coins will be refunded to the respective users after a fixed time
