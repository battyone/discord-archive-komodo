dimxy 06-Aug-19 05:49 PM
I'm rethinking about issues with staking. Actually my 3-node marmara chain is stalling. This shows itself as blocks are generated with long delays and there are messages in log:
640 seconds until elegible, waiting. 600 seconds until elegible, waiting. ...
(That is, more than 10 min waiting for an utxo.) Our other developers suggested that this is because not enough utxos for staking. Indeed, in non-marmara chains we usually use utxo splitting on 64 addresses (this is about normal coins of course). In our marmara chain we have not yet implemented such splitting for activated coins, and I'm thinking why we need this. As I understand JL correctly, in large marmara chains we would not need splitting as there will be many user addresses and segids will be filled up naturally. In fact this splitting on 64 segids is needed for small chains, while we do not have many addresses yet. So I am implementing this splitting of activated coins for small test chains to run stable (And we should understand it would affect rewards generation: of course, split activated coins will have much more advantage over non-split lock-in-loop coins). Seems, this feature will be used also in the production chain on its early stage to make it stable. And it should be some transition period until enough addresses appear on chain because the initially split coins would affect new users' activated and lock-in-loop coins rewards. (That is, the initially split coins should be taken off as enough other addresses are on the chain)
