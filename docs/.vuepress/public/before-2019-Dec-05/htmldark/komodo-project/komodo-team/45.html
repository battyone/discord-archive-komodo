Just some more info about the rebrand, since a lot of people have been asking about what exactly it will include. We don't have specifics or details yet but one of the major things we're trying to improve is the messaging. We want to find ways to talk about Komodo that are both easy to understand and really do justice to the magnitude of the tech itself. That's a hard task, as explaining Komodo is complicated. We are also working on the "user journey", which will help educate people about Komodo and guide them through our ecosystem in the way that makes the most sense. We also need to figure out what we want out CTA (call to action) to be, which is tough because it may be different things for different people. If you're a developer, we want you to build on Komodo. But even that isn't clear because devs can build in different ways (do we want devs to launch a blockchain? start coding smart contracts? building dApps? or something else??) If you're an entrepreneur or business leader, we want you to use Komodo tech but you can't build yourself so we would need the CTA to be contacting ChainZilla or Chainmakers. If you're a user, the CTA might be downloading Agama, downloading HypderDEX, buying KMD, etc. So we need to figure all of this out and really craft the messaging and website in a way that caters to everyone while also pursuing the goals we've decided help Komodo the most.
6
We're also working to establish a clear product strategy. This will, at some point, need to come with a legal business entity which will work closely with Komodo but still be separate. This is necessary for a few reasons. First, the legal entity will allow us to raise funds from investors and VCs. Second, it will help to build new partnerships and agreements with other projects, businesses, educational institutions, etc. Third, the legal entity will leverage the product strategy to develop revenue models, which will sustain the project well into the future. (edited)
And of course, once all of this happens, the marketing team will help promote the CTAs that support the product strategy and revenue streams
So lots and lots of stuff going on behind the scenes. The new visual identity is just the "front end" of the rebrand

@jorian crowdsourcing ideas doesn't always mean you have to use them verbatim. It's a seeding ground. Let me quote feverbee who are a good source of community ideas that I read regularly. "Crowdsourced competitions are widely misunderstood. Visit any major crowdsource effort and you will find only a tiny percentage of ideas are ever implemented. Most contributors don’t have the skill, knowledge of your products, or understand your company’s constraints well enough to develop a fully-formed idea. Worse yet, crowdsourcing efforts are rarely set up to accommodate truly groundbreaking ideas. Browse the 550+ ideas implemented through Dell’s Ideastorm, is there really a game-changer in the bunch? Most ideas tinkered around the edges of old products. There aren’t any new smartphones, social networks, cryptocurrencies, machine learning, or breakthrough products here. Crowdsourced ideas often closely resemble a list of customer complaints (isn’t every complaint also an idea of what to improve?) But the real benefit of crowdsourcing ideas and solutions extends beyond fully-formed ideas. It helps identify amazing talent to hire, new approaches to follow and to confirm or refute your existing thinking. You might not use an idea in its entirety, but you may learn from unique aspects of each idea. Having a hundred, or even a thousand, fresh pair of eyes give their opinion on a problem more than pays for the relatively tiny fee to attract them in the first place." - https://www.feverbee.com/crowdsource/

Steve Lee 07-Sep-18 08:34 PM
@jorian @Everything Tangle I just wanted to clarify the reasoning and logic behind the infographic bounty contest. Could our team and/or Ideas by Nature produce this infographic? Absolutely. However, getting the design of an infographic isn’t the main goal of this bounty contest. The main goal is to educate people outside of our existing community and leverage their networks to promote increased awareness and growth. Although the bounty is open and shared within our own community, we’ve also identified finalists from bounty contests ran by other projects. We’re also reaching out to crypto agencies, research organizations, and freelance content contributors that might not be familiar with Komodo. We’ve also kept the guidelines open-ended and the resource material fairly dense, so it forces these content creators to really understand and learn our technology and vision. This will push them to creatively reinterpret the messaging in ways we might have thought of versus copy pasting and adding a stock image. Creating the infographic ourselves and only promoting it within our own community is like preaching into an echo chamber and can also be seen as biased. However, if people are seeing 10-30 different infographics all talking about KMD, it might entice people to really look into us and tell their friends if the messaging resonates. Our hopes are to introduce KMD to a whole new audience as well as reintroduce the new KMD to those who might have not stayed up to date. This bounty will serve as a pilot on how effective this sort of approach could be.
