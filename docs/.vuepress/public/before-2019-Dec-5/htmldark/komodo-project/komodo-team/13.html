Steve Lee 29-Jun-18 05:14 PM
In regards to the explanation of independent chains we've been employing two analogies.
1) Other platforms/eth are like a giant highway dapps as cars. When there were less cars its was fine, but as the influx of cars exceeded the limited lanes in the highway traffic becomes inevitable.
KMD gives you your own dedicated private highway. If you outgrow that, we can add another lane (scaling solution) on demand. We can continue to do this to scale linearly.
2) If you lived in an apt complex, would you pay for internet that was being shared by the entire building? Eth/other platforms is like sharing a single wifi/broadband connection and being affected by other people streaming, gaming, or downloading etc
KMD gives you your own dedicated wifi and internet per unit. If bandwidth usage exceeds you can always upgrade (scaling). This has no limit.
we've used the road one a lot with influencers and research groups and press and was well received.

Audo 30-Jun-18 12:49 PM
Thank you for the ideas @jorian , they are good ones. There definitely is room to improve for us. We should produce content in more regular interwals and maybe our focus on a single campaign has made us blind to some of the things we could have blogged about. Many times, however, we need a bit more time to write about something and often we have hard time understanding the technical aspects and angles. Example 1: You suggested a blog about "the creation of several third party ecosystem developers, like DEVSEC, ChainZilla etc." – This is definitely a blog we will write. The situation is very young still, however. These businesses are just setting themselves up. Also our business development team is planning how we handle this exactly. Thus it's better to wait for the situation to mature, at least in my opinion. A blog about this in 1 month will be just as powerful as it would be today. Example 2: "someone doing a write-up about what the consequences of the ASIC for KMD are" – sounds interesting, but I'm not sure what this is about. Our marketing team needs a better bridge to the technical minds who would feed us content. Indeend, @jorian if you could help us in this perhaps we could talk. We are looking people like you. Example 3: - "someone doing a write-up about what ZEC is doing and why or why not KMD is integrating their updates" – the same as above, our resources are limited and it takes a long time for us to understand these in enough detail to blog about them. We need a dedicated technical person to help us out.
"the importance of GAME using dPoW (or maybe after a couple of months to test the time a bit)" – A team decision was made not to draw too much attention to our dPoW promise as of yet, because it's in PEN testing and so on. Thus our marketing team has only been able to retweet mentions and not directly blog about it. We will be making a blog post about this in time once we are ready to market this feature. It will still work as we can interview GAME and explain that they have been using our security service for 3 months without any attacks, and so on.
"crypto conditions, scaling and all that has been going on in #dev-scalability" – We have been giving the stress test small mentions here and there. However, it has been a conscious decision on our part not to make too much noise about it until the numbers have been validated and the infrastructure is ready. We haven't had any timeline on how long it takes to setup the infrastructure and it is better to make the noice once we know for sure when the actual live marketable stress test will happen. "(a short summary of) the advancements in CHIPS and the creation of the CHIPS community" – Good point, we should, but there is no rush. CHIPS project was started with the decision to have ZERO marketing for it.
'All in all let me just sum up this to two points: 1) Yes, our marketing could do better job on few things. We are improving and now we want to get you guys more involved. 2) One problem we have indentified is that Komodo is all over the place – and with that we mean the messages we send out through marketing We are trying to focus on one talking point at a time and build campaigns. Since the ICO our marketing has been reactionary but we want to get to a position when we can plan and anticipate. That means that we are not going to talk about all the "noice" that is going on before it is mature enough to be properly marketed (i.e. CHIPS and Assetchain businesses).
Then what do we talk about today? Right now we started our "Komodo Evolution" campaign in which we revealed our 5 technology pillars. We will talk about security, scalability, interoperability, privacy, and simplicity – and how a blockchain platform/ecosystem needs them all. These are the 5 main selling points why a business should use Komodo instead of some other blockchain/technology provider. From this campaign it is natural to transition more towards a) scalability test marketing – including some short of an online event with a scalability test demonstration b) announcing how to get started with Komodo through the 3rd party businesses
