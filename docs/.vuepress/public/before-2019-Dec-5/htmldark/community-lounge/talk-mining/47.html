@yorklab the key to answering the question is you knowing what you want long-term and if you can afford scale. If you want some kind of in-and-out, then you'd be better off with newer GPUs to give you some "mobility" as to what you can do next. If you want to stay with a specific algo "until the wheels fall off", then ASICs will always be a good choice. If the latter, then sooner is always better. Scale of ASICs (or any leading edge hw) is best done with the "as much as you can, as fast as you can" mentality. If it takes 2 months to reach positive ROI on deviceX and you can get 12 of them, then you can get 12 more in 2 months. If you only get 1 at a time, then your growth rate will be exponentially slower. My personal philosophy, as cliche as it is, is "go big or go home".
