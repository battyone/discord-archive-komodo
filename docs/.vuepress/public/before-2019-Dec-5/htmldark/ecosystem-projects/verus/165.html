[dscrdvrsc] <Oink> Some explanation on OTC and escrow: OTC stands for Over The Counter, which basically means you trade directly with your counterpart that wants to trade with you. The risk involved in this is you have to trust your counterpart to send his coin, after you send yours and vice versa. Escrow means you hand your funds over to a third party after you agree with your counterpart to a trade. Your trade partner does the same and as soon as the 3rd party receives the agreed funds from both trade partners, the traded funds are released to the traders (after a fee is applied), thus eliminating the risk in being scammed. If no payment is received from either trade partner, your funds will be return after a set period of time (edited)
[dscrdvrsc] <Oink> In short: your funds don’t sit endlessly on an exchange where your funds are potentially vulnerable, they stay in your own wallet until you make a trade, safely under your own control and you’re secured by escrow once you make the trade.
