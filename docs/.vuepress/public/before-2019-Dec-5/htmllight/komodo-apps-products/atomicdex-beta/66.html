artem.pikulin 18-Sep-19 06:49 AM
Also worth to add that only spending transactions are left unconfirmed, in more details: 1. Maker sends payment, taker waits for this payment to be confirmed so double spending is prevented here. 2. Taker then sends his payment, maker waits until this payment is confirmed before attempt to spend it. 3. Only then maker is spending taker's payment revealing the secret and taker spends maker's payment. @ITM Only the spending stages are left unconfirmed in this case, the inputs being spent on this stage are hash-timelocked so other side won't be able to spend the transaction until timelock conditions are met (few hours).

artem.pikulin 18-Sep-19 06:56 AM
These transactions were spends of swap payments that were confirmed before.
The protocol is as follows: 1. Maker sends payment that protected with hash of secret. We must wait for it to be confirmed. 2. Taker then sends his payment using same hash of secret. We must wait for this to be confirmed. 3. Maker reveals secret to spent the Taker's payment - we don't have to wait for this to be confirmed. The taker won't be able to refund it for long time. 4. Taker uses the secret to spend the payment. Same here, to make a refund attempt Maker needs to wait for long time, but since it passes the transaction will be confirmed.
