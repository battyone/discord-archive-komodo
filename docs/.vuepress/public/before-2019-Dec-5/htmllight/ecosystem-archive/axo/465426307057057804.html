axo777 02-Nov-18 07:09 PM
@Hermes We are building a centralized/decentralized exchange that has its own native currency -- AXO. With our AXOCoin we can expand into many other markets in Mexico in the future. Next year we will be getting our Mexican FINCEN license where we can issue our own debit card, among other things. Until Mexico liberalizes its foreign exchange regulations the exchange and its Peso Gateway has to operate exclusively within Mexico. Today a Mexican resident can only have a bank account in Mexican Pesos. That is why we set up AXOBIT SAPI DE CV as the Mexican legal entity to develop the cryptocurrency market in Mexico. About half of Mexico's population lives without a bank account which makes for a very fertile market. Also, those people do not trust banks very much. There is a lot of interest in cryptocurrencies in Mexico, that is why we got about 8,000 followers on Facebook in just a couple of weeks. AXOBIT SAPI will be able to expand into several Latin American markets with relative ease downstream. However, since Mexico is a "silo" we have also set up AXO LLC, a legal entity in Wyoming to access the US Dollar market. AXO LLC is currently developing a Blockchain as a Service (BaaS) product to track things from origin to kitchen in the food sector since it is a "low hanging fruit". This will be done with RFID and CC Smart Contract Oracle technology . You may have heard about Walmarts experience where they were able to reduce the time it took them track where their avocados were in their supply chain from 6 days to just a few seconds. This was done with IBM and HyperLedger Fabric which is a private distributed ledger integrated with a private blockchain that is very, very expensive. AXO LLC is in negotiation with a NASDAQ company to provide location signals to our Oracle that will be orders of magnitude cheaper and better than the IBM product. HyperLedger recently added ETH with a wrapper to allow EC-20 contracts. (edited)
