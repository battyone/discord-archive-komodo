[dscrdvrsc] <miketout> Hi @dude, The reason I think someone who cares about building an application might choose to use PBaaS is the same reason we chose to make a new blockchain to solve key challenges in crypto. We wanted an independent chain with its own rules and capabilities that simply didn't exist. We wanted to leverage what we felt was the most secure option at the time, Komodo with dPoW and to enhance security and capability over the base starting point. We wanted a way to provision PBaaS chains for the applications described in the vision paper, and no decentralized solutions existed. We have already developed some of the most secure new blockchain protocols and foundation in use today for public, truly decentralized P2P systems. In the process, we have leveraged and appropriately enhanced Komodo's crypto-condition technology that is behind Stake Guard, paving the way for what we need to develop PBaaS crypto conditions. There's still work to do to get to PBaaS chains, but when we do, we expect Verus applications to have their own blockchain "classes" with their own rules and crypto-conditions that serve and scale the application. There's not a great limit to what kinds of applications can be built that way, but it's a very different model to one chain, one set of rules, all applications share a VM.
