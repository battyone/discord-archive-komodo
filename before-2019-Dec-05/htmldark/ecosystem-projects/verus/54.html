This is because Verus uses default keypool size of 100 (just like zcash), which means when your wallet gets created, 100 R-addresses are generated just for you and stored in the wallet. If you solo mine, your rewards will eventually land on those addresses. Thats just how the wallet is designed, it starts with 100 semi-hidden addresses despite showing you just one in Agama Receive tab. WIF is not exacly a private key, it's a way to present them in a nice way so that they could be easily copied and moved around. But it translates to you private key indeed, its just another way of representing your original private key. Check up https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Wallet_import_format (edited)
